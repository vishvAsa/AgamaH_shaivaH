The system of guhyakAlI is presented in a rather complete form in the sprawling eponymous khaNDa of the even more massive mahAkAla-saMhitA. 

## uttarAmnAya?
While this system can technically be placed in the uttarAmnAya – the kAlI-centric systems – its origin is poorly known. It does not show any specific affinities to the classical uttarAmnAya of the bhAnavI-kaulinI-based system or the ancient teachings such as the sUtra-s of vAtUlanAtha. 

## ki~nchita-tantra
Among the fragmentary tantric material from Nepal is a text titled the “ki~nchita-tantra.” It remains unclear if this was its original name. Apart from the apANinIya register of the language, this text is both fragmentary and corrupt. 

Despite these, we can see in it rather original material with clear connections to the guhyakAlI tradition. We suggest that this is hence an earlier text of this tradition than the guhyakAlI khaNDa and probably lies closer to the root of this tradition.

## cremation ground worship
\* It has a clear association with the cremation ground worship of the bhairava and his consort (siddhi)bhairavI, who is identified with guhyakAlI. The word bhairava is mentioned 31 times in the surviving fragment along with a bhairava-shikhA mantra: aiM shrIM phreM bhairavAya haiM hUM phaT namaH || 

The bhairava teaches, and sanctions the practice of the lore, and the goddess is invoked with the bhairava as trailokyamAtR^i: 

bhairava-sahita-trailokya-mAtre hreM cheM CheM namaH svAhA ||

\* Foreshadowing the classic polyzoocephalous form of the goddess guhyakAlI, in a long mantra, the mahAbhairavI is addressed as: 

1. siMha-shArdUla-vyAghrAdinI; 
2. mR^igeshvarI; 
3. sarva-pakShIrAjA-rAj~nI; 
4. makara-kumbhIra-machChakA (crocodile and gharial); 
5. kachChapa-machChAdiShu rAj~nI (the queen among the tortoises and fishes).

\* The surviving fragment mentions the pITha-s of jAlandhara, kAmarUpa and devIkoTa but not the others (oDDiyAna is missing!)

\* There is a clear connection to the old cult of chAmuNDA with her companion group of aShTamAtR^ikA-s. The surviving fragment identifies the goddess of the system with chAmuNDA and gives at least 10 chAmuNDA mantra-s. She is termed siddhichAmuNDA, siddhikAlI and mahAvajreshvarI. In one mantra she is mentioned as being worshiped at the jAlandhara pITha. In one place, the goddess is also identified with pratya~NgirA.

\* The yoginI-s are mentioned at least 20 times in the surviving fragment. Of these, there are multiple references to the conference of human yoginI-s (yoginI-melApaM karoti |). However, the bhairavI/kAlI is invoked in the midst of the cluster of yoginI-s in multiple mantra-s. One of these is known as the sarvayoginInAM samaya-vidyA. Whereas DAkinI-s are never mentioned, shAkinI-s are mentioned a few times.

\* A guhyakAlI yantra is mentioned as having 1. A trikoNA; 2. A pa~nchAra (pentagram); 3. ShaTkoNa (hexagram); 4. aShTAra (8 star); 5. dashapatrANi (10-petalled lotus).

\* The text specifies an Avesha ritual, wherein the deployment of a mantra to the goddess identified with siddhikarAlI results in goddess possession (evidently of a young woman). The possessed individual displays various indications of possession, including signs of the aShTamAtR^ikA-s: e.g., 

> “mA bhaiShI vadati |  
> ekA~NgulaM tarjanIM darshayati | …  
> rudrANI a~NguShThena |  
> vaiShNavI vAma-madhyamayA |  
> indrANI anAmikayA |…”
