A key maNDala of the pashchimAmnAya is the vajra-gahvara-prastAra. The 11 levels are believed to represent the 11 rudra-s. The 7 columns are the 7 mAtR^ikA-s. In the intersection of the atharvan tradition with the pashchimAmnaya, the prastAra may be deployed for apotropaic functions using the below atharvan incantation. 

The deity of the prastAra is vajrakubjIshvarI or vajriNI a "kaulized" indrAnI. 

Before drawing it, he should have a human yoginI of his kula lead the initial pUja. Then he installs it with his own kulA~NganA with the guidance of the kulAcharya. This is the simplest of the vajra-prastAra -- the gahvara. 

There are more complex ones, which are secret maNDala-s of tradition drawn after visualizing kubjikA at the four pITha-s. 

He starts with the oDDiyAna-pITha and invokes the goddess as raktA and conceives her emanating ferocious daughter goddesses with a bovine face, an ape-face and others in different parts of the body. 

Then she is invoked at jAlandhara in the form of a kAlI-like goddess karAlinI, 

then at pUrNagiri as chaNDAkShI -- a solar-coded pUrvAmnAya-like goddess, 

and finally as kAmeshvarI at kAmarUpa. 

Finally, she is invoked at a 5th pITha known as mAta~Nga, unique this yAga, where she emanates 9 daughter goddesses starting with hArikA and 9 kumAra-s and 5 gaNa-s starting with heraMbha.
