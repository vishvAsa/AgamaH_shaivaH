The pashchimAmnAya originally had a rather simple conception of the saMvartAmaNDala in the ShaT-sAhasra-saMhitA:

> त्रिकोणं च चतुष्कोणं  
षट्कोणं चैव बाह्यतः ।  
तत्रोत्पन्नं जगत् सर्वं  
तत्र लीनं चराचरम् ॥  
वडवानल-मध्यस्थं  
संवर्तामण्डलं तु तम् ।

> There is a triangle, a square and a hexagon (hexastar) going outwards. There originates the entire world, and therein is retracted the animate and inanimate. Standing in the midst of the equine fire, this is indeed the saMvartAmaNDala.

In the dakShiNAmnAya’s yoginI-hR^idaya section of the vAmakeshvara tantra, a similar view is conceived for shrIchakra. 

The simple saMvartAmaNDala is conceived as a triangle enclosed in a square in turn enclosed by a hexastar. Such a conception has something of a parallel among the Greeks in the form of the Platonic tetractys culminating in the tetrahedron (also seen in the pashchimAmnaya as the shR^i~NgATaka).
