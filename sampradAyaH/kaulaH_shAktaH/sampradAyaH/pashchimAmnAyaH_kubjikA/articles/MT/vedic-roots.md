The samAvedic brAhmaNa-s have a cryptic allusion to the deva-s having supported the sun in the sky with sarasvatI. However, it collapsed, and hence it is supposed to be full of curves (tasmAt sA kubjimatIva) -- a river with many bends. This was an attempt at drawing a sambandha between the heavenly sarasvatI -- the Milky Way -- and an earthly river.

An "out there" proposal:  
Origin of the name kubjikA for the pashchimAmnAya goddess has been a bit of a mystery. She is only rarely shown with the stooping (kubja) iconography. Instead, we hold that it relates to her curve form as the kuNDalinI in the mUlAdhara.+++(4)+++ 

In light of this, it is notable that rather early on in the shruti itself we see the term kubjimatI for a river often identified with the great transfunctional goddess. 

It has been proposed that kubja- root in kubjikA is an Aryanization of a non-Aryan substratal word, much like chAmuNDA. However, we suggest that it might instead simply have a deeper Indo-Aryan provenance via the curves of the river goddess sarasvatI kubjimatI worshipped in the sArasvata sattra. 

Per this hypothesis, this concept of a river goddess full of curves was transposed in lost memory to the coiled kuNDalinI and later remerged as the pashchimAmnaya goddess' name.
