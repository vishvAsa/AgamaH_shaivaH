अथ सप्तममाह्निकम् ।

English

Chapter Seven ….

अथ देशाध्वा ।

English

The Path of Space

परं संवेदनम्

तत्र समस्त एव अयं मूर्ति-वैचित्र्याभासन-शक्ति-जो देशाध्वा
संविदि विश्रान्तः।

विस्तारः (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)

प्राच्यभागे कालाध्वा उत्तरे तु देशाध्वा इति कालाध्वानमुक्त्वा
देशाध्वानं वक्तुम् उपक्रमते तत्रेति ।

पदार्थस्य स्वं रूपं मूर्तिः ।

English

The entire ‘path of space’ (desādhva) 1 born of śakti, which creates diverse forms, rests in consciousness.

तद्+++(→संविद्)+++-द्वारेण
+++(तत्सृष्टे)+++ शून्ये बुद्धौ, प्राणे,
नाडी-चक्रानुचक्रेषु, बहिः शरीरे यावल् लिङ्ग-स्थण्डिल-प्रतिमादौ
समस्तो ऽध्वा परिनिष्ठितः।

विस्तारः (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)

तद्द्वारेण - संविद्द्वारेण
तत्सृष्टे शून्यादाव् अध्वा स्थितः । यदुक्तम्

अध्वा समस्त एवायं
चिन्मात्रे संव्यवस्थितः ।
यत् तत्र नहि विश्रान्तं
तन् नभः-कुसुमायते ॥

इति ।

English

This entire path, through the outlet of consciousness, 2 attains its extreme external limit in the void (sūnya), intellect, prāṇa, and the groups of chan nels and subchannels. Then it expands externally in the body and (all the way) to the extent of the linga, altar, and even the image of deity, etc.

तं समस्तम् अध्वानं
देहे विलाप्य,
देहं च प्राणे,
तं धियि,
तां शून्ये,
तत्-संवेदने निर्भर-परिपूर्ण-संवित् सम्पद्यते।

विस्तारः (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)

यदुक्तम्

पृथिवी कठिनत्वेन
द्रव्यत्वेऽम्भः प्रकीर्तितम् ।

इत्यादि नीत्या समस्तोऽध्वा देहलीन

इति ।

English

The knower of the truth of the real nature of the thirty-six tattvas first dissolves all these paths into the body, [then from] the body into prāṇa, prāṇa into intellect, intellect into void, and finally void into consciousness. In this way, this kind of yogin becomes permeated with consciousness not dependent on any thing external.

षट्-त्रिंशत्-तत्त्व-स्वरूप-ज्ञः
तद्-उत्तीर्णां संविदं परम-शिव-रूपां पश्यन्
विश्वमयीम् अपि संवेदयेत।

English

The knower of the truth of the real nature of the thirty-six tattvas. Then he experiences consciousness as Paramaśiva, and transcending even that state, he experiences himself as being immanent as well.3

अपरमार्गः

अपरथा वेद्य-भागम् एव कञ्चित्
परत्वेन गृह्णीयात्,
माया-गर्भाधिकारिणं विष्णु-ब्रह्मादिकं वा।

English

However, in a lesser way (if one is unable to experience the fullness of consciousness), one may take up a portion of know ables such as Brahmā or Viṣnu, the superintending deities of the sphere of māyā, as the highest.

प्रक्रिया

तस्माद् अवश्यं प्रक्रिया-ज्ञान-परेण भवितव्यम् ।
तदुक्तं “न प्रक्रियापरं ज्ञानम्” इति ।

English

In this way, one should become well acquainted with the knowledge of prakriyā. As it is said: “There is no knowledge superior to prakriyā."

तत्त्वानि

तत्र पृथिवी-तत्त्वं शत-कोटि-प्रविस्तीर्णं,
ब्रह्माण्ड-गोलक-रूपम्।

विस्तारः (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)

ब्रह्मणः सम्बन्धि अण्डं वस्तुपिण्डो ऽण्डम् उच्यते
इति निरुक्तस्थित्या
वस्तूनां तन्व्-अक्षादीनां पिण्डः समूहो ऽण्डं -
तद् एव गोलकं च ।

प्रथमं काञ्चनं प्रोक्तं
रौक्मं चैव द्वितीयकम् ।
ताम्रं च लोहजं चैव
क्रमाद् एव व्यवस्थितम् ॥

इत्य्-एवम्-आदीन्य् अण्डानि ।

English

The following is the scheme of prakriyā:
in the path of space, the expansion of the earth principle (prthivītattva) extends one hundred crores in length. It is spherical like the egg of Brahmā (Brahmānda).4

तस्य +अन्तः
कालाग्निर्, नरकाः, पातालानि, पृथिवी, स्वर्गो यावद् ब्रह्मलोक
इति ।

English

[[102]] At the lower end of that Brahmānda is
the ‘fire of time’ (kālāgni),
followed by hell, the nether worlds, earth, and heaven, which extend to the world of Brahmā.

ब्रह्माण्ड-बाह्ये रुद्राणां शतम् ।
न च ब्रह्माण्डानां सङ्ख्या विद्यते ।

English

Outside the egg of Brahmā are the worlds of the hundred rudras (satarudras).5 There is no fixed number of Brahmā’s worlds.

ततो धरा-तत्त्वात् दश-गुणं जलतत्त्वम् ।
तत उत्तरोत्तरं दश-गुणम् अहङ्कारान्तम् +++(प्रत्येकं तत्त्वम्)+++ ।

English

The principle called water (jala) is ten times the size of the earth. In this way, each subsequent principle ending with egoity (ahamkāra) is ten times the size of the previous tattva.6

तद् यथा -
जलं, तेजो, वायुर्, नभः,
तन्-मात्र-पञ्चक+अक्षैकादश+++(←ज्ञान-कर्मेन्द्रिय-मनांसि)+++-गर्भो ऽहङ्कारश् चेति ।

विस्तारः (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)

यद् उक्तं

आकाशावरणाद् ऊर्ध्वम्
अहङ्काराद् अधः पुनः ।
तन्मात्रादि-मनोन्तानां
पुराणि शिव-शासने ॥

इति ।

English

These tattvas are: water, fire, air, ākāśa, the five subtle elements (tanmātras), the five motor organs, the five sense organs, the mind (manas), and finally the ego (ahamkāra).

अहङ्कारात् शतगुणं बुद्धितत्त्वम्।

English

The principle of intellect or buddhi tattva7 is one hundred times the size of ’egoity’ (ahaṁkāra).

[[६५]]
ततः सहस्रधा प्रकृति-तत्त्वम्।
एतावत् प्रकृत्य्-अण्डम् ।
तच् च ब्रह्माण्डवत् असङ्ख्यम् ।

English

The principle of primodial nature, or prakr̥ti, is one thousand times more extensive than the buddhi. This is the extent of the sphere of prakr̥ti (prakr̥ti aṇḍa) 8. The spheres of prakr̥ti are also innumerable, just like the spheres of Brahmā.

प्रकृतितत्त्वात् पुरुषतत्त्वं च दशसहस्रधा ।
पुरुषात् नियतिः लक्षधा ।

English

The puruṣa principle is ten thousand times larger than the principle of prakr̥ti. The principle of ’natural law,’ or niyati, is one hundred thousand times larger than that of puruṣa.

नियतेर् उत्तरोत्तरं दश-लक्ष-धा +++(प्रति-तत्त्वम्)+++ कला-तत्त्वान्तम् ।
तद् यथा -
नियतिः, रागः, अशुद्ध-विद्या, कालः, कला चेति ।

English

From niyati up to the principle of limited doership, or kalā, each subsequent principle is one hundred thousand times larger than the preceding one.
These principles are ’natural law’ (niyati), ‘attachment’ (rāga). ‘impure knowledge’ (aśuddhavidyā), ’time’ (kāla), and ’limited doership’ (kalā).

कला-तत्त्वात् कोटिधा माया।
एतावत् मायाण्डम् ।

English

The māyā principle is ten million times larger than kalā. This makes up the ‘sphere of māyā’ (māyānda).

मायातत्त्वात् शुद्ध-विद्या दश-कोटि-गुणिता ।
विद्या-तत्त्वात् ईश्वरतत्त्वं शत-कोटि-धा ।

English

The principle of ‘pure knowledge’ (śuddhavidyā) is ten million times larger than māyā. The īśvara principle is ten million times the size of śuddhavidyā,

ईश्वरतत्त्वात् सादाख्यं सहस्रकोटिधा।
+++([क्: सादाशिवाख्यं सहस्रकोटिधा सादाशिवाख्यादिति पाठः ।])+++

English

while the sadāśiva principle is one billion times the size of the iśvara principle. The śakti tattva is one billion times larger than sadāśiva.

सादाख्यात् वृन्द-गुणितं शक्ति-तत्त्वम्।
इति शक्त्य्-अण्डम् ।

English

This is known as the ‘sphere of śakti’ (śaktiaṇḍa).

सा शक्तिर् व्याप्य यतो विश्वम् अध्वानम् अन्तर् बहिर् आस्ते
तस्मात् व्यापिनी ।

English

As this śakti exists pervading all paths externally and internally, it is called vyāpini.

एवम् एतानि +उत्तरोत्तरम् आवरणतया वर्तमानानि तत्त्वानि
उत्तरं व्यापकं, पूर्वं व्याप्यम्
इति स्थित्या वर्तन्ते ।

English

All these subsequent principles exist as the sheaths of the prior one. [[103]] The subsequent one is pervasive and the prior one is pervaded.

यावद् अशेष-शक्ति-तत्वान्तो ऽध्वा
शिवतत्त्वेन व्याप्तः ।
शिवतत्त्वं पुनर् अप्रमेयं सर्वाध्वोत्तीर्णं, सर्वाध्वव्यापकं च ।

English

However, all of the paths, ending with śakti, are pervaded by śiva.
Furthermore, śivatattva, being immeasurable, transcends and pervades all six paths.

तत्त्वपतयः

एतत् तत्त्वान्तराल-वर्तीनि यानि भुवनानि,
तत्-पतय एव
अत्र पृथिव्यां स्थिता इति ।

English

The Lords of the worlds who exist in the intervening regions of each principle also have their abode on this earth.

[[६६]] तेषु आयतनेषु ये म्रियन्ते
तेषां तत्र तत्र गतिं ते वितरन्ति ।
क्रमाच् च +ऊर्ध्वोर्ध्वं प्रेरयन्ति दीक्षाक्रमेण ।

English

Those who died in these places are offered respective places by the Lords. Then they lead them from one lower region to the upper one by means of the procedure of initiation.

तद् यथा

कालाग्निः, कूष्माण्डो
नरकेशो, हाटकोऽथ भूतलपः, ।
ब्रह्मा मुनिलोकेशो,
रुद्रः पञ्चाण्ड-मध्यगतः ॥

English

The fire of time, Kuṣumānda, the Lord of the nether world, Hataka, the cauldron of the earth, Brahma, Muni, Lokesa, and Rudras reside in the intermediate regions of five spheres (aṇḍas).

अधरे ऽनन्तः प्राच्या
कपालि-वह्न्य्-अन्त+++(=यम)+++-निर्ऋति-बालाख्याः +++(इति पश्चिमान्तम्)+++ ।
+++(वायव्यात्)+++ लघु–निधि-पति–विद्याधिप-
शम्भूर्ध्वान्तं, स वीर-भद्र-पतिः ॥

English

Below Ananta exists Kapalin, Agni, Yama, Nirrti, Bala, Sigra, Nidhisvara, the Lord of vidyās-śambhu, and at the end of the head is Vīrabhadra.

इति षोडश-पुरम् एतत्-
पार्थिवम् अण्डं निवृत्ति-कला ।

English

These sixteen worlds are included in the sphere of the earth, and kalā is nivrtti (which is the element that gives birth to the sphere).

लकुलीश–भार-भूतिर्
दिण्ड्य् आषाढी च पुष्कर-निमेषौ ॥

प्रभास-सुरेशाव् इति
सलिले प्रत्यात्मकाष्टकं प्रोक्तम् ।

English

These eight are called the Lords of water: Lakulīśa, Bhārabhuti, Dindi, Asadhi, Puskara, Nimesa, Prabhāsa, and Sureśa.

भैरव-केदार–महा-
काला मध्यास्र-जल्पाख्याः ॥

श्रीशैल–हरिश्-चन्द्राव्
इति गुह्याष्टकम् इदं महसि ।

English

Bhairava, Kedāra, Mahākāla, Madhyāmra, Jalpa, SriSaila, and Hariscandra are called Guhyāstaka, the eight secret gods who exist in the principle of fire.

भीमेन्द्राट्टाः स-विमल-
कनखल-नाखल–कुरु-स्थिति–गयाख्याः ॥

[[६७]]

अति-गुह्याष्ट्कम् एतन्
मरुति सतन्मात्रके साक्षे ।

English

The group of eight highly secret gods is situated in the principle of air in the instrument of knowledge along with tanmatras. They are: Bhima, Indra, Attahasa, Vimala, Kanakhala, Nakhala, Kuruksetra, and Gaya.

स्थाणु-सुवर्णाख्यौ किल
भद्रो गोकर्णको महालयकः ॥

अविमुक्त-रुद्रकोटी
वस्त्रापद इत्य् अदः पवित्रं खे ।

English

Sthānu, Suvarṇa, Bhadra, Gokarna, Mahālaya, Avi mukta, Rudrakoti, and Vastrapada [this group of] the pure eight Lords, exist in the principle of the ether (void).

स्थूल-स्थूलेश–शङ्कु-
श्रुति–कालाश् चाथ मण्डल-भृत् ॥

माकोटाण्ड-द्वितय-
च्छगलाण्डाष्टकं त्व् अहङ्कारे ।

English

Sthūla, Shūleśvara, Sankukarṇa, Kālañjara, Maṇḍala-bhịt, Mākoṭa, Duraṇḍa and Chagalāṇḍa, [these eight] remain in the principle called ego (ahamkāra).

अन्ये त्व् अहङ्कारान्तास्
तन्मात्राणीन्द्रियाणि चेत्याहुः ॥

English

According to the view of some, however, the subtle elements and sense organs have their location in the principle of ego (ahamkāra).

धियि देवयोनयोऽष्टौ
प्रकृतौ योगाष्टकं किलाकृत-प्रभृति ।
इति +++(पूर्वोक्ताः)+++ सप्ताष्टक+++(=७*८-पति-)+++-भुवना
प्रतिष्ठितिः सलिलतस् तु मूलान्ता ॥

English

In the principle called intellect, the group of eight heavenly born beings is located, while in prakr̥ti the group of eight yogas beginning with akr̥ta, etc., is found. 9

These ‘worlds of seven groups of eight beings’ resting in the kalā named pratiṣthā are located in the water principle and are pervaded up to prakr̥ti.

नरि+++(=पुरुषे)+++ वामाद्या रुद्रा
एकादश, +++(अशुद्ध-)+++वित्-कला-नियतिषु स्यात् ।
प्रत्य्-एकं भुवन-द्वयम्,
अथ काले तत् त्रयं, निशायां+++(=मायायां)+++ स्युः …॥
… अष्टाव्, अष्टाविंशति-
भुवना विद्या नरान् निशान्तम् इयम् ।
+++(रागतत्त्वय् किमपि नोच्यते??)+++

English

In the puruṣa principle are located10 the worlds of the eleven rudras called vāma and so on.
The principles of impure vidyā, kalā, and niyati have two worlds each,11 while the principle kala has three, and the principle māyā … … has eight worlds. 12

Thus, from puruṣa to māyā the number of worlds is twenty-eight.

विद्यायां पञ्च स्युर्,
विद्येशाष्टकम् अथैश्वरे तत्त्वे ॥

विस्तारः (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)

एवं सर्वेषां भुवनानां सङ्ख्या
अष्टादशोत्तरं शतं यदुक्तम् ।

एवं तु सर्वतत्त्वेषु शतमष्टादशोत्तरम् ।
भुवनानां परिज्ञेयं सङ्क्षेपान्न तु विस्तरात् ॥ इति ।

English

In pure vidyā there are five worlds, 13 and in the iśvara principle the number [of worlds] belonging to the Lords of vidyās is eight.

सादाख्ये पञ्चकम् इति
अष्टादश-भुवनिका शान्ता
+++(किमत्राधिकं तत्त्वम्?? कलातत्त्वं पूर्वम् एव गतम्। )+++।

English

In the principle called sadākhya there are five worlds, while in the kalā сalled sāntā there are eighteen worlds.14

अध्वानम् इमं सकलं
देहे प्राणेऽथ धियि महा-नभसि ॥
संविदि च परं पश्यन्-
पूर्णत्वाद् भैरवी भवति ॥

English

Conceiving all these paths (adhvas) either in the body, or in the vital energy, or in the intellect, or in the great void of consciousness as the highest, one attains the fullness of Bhairava.

परमेसर-सासण-सुणिरुइउ

झहुज्झति-सरीरि-पवणि
संवे-अ णि-अपेक्खन्त-उ पहुर-इ परि-उण्णु ॥

संविदि निरीक्ष्य पश्यति परमेशम् ॥

इति श्रिमदभिनवगुप्ताचार्यविरचिते तन्त्रसारे देशाध्वप्रकाशनं नाम
सप्तममाह्निकम् ॥७॥

English

Here ends Chapter Seven of the Tantrasāra of Abhinavagupta revealing the nature of the path of space.

  1. According to this system, the Lord’s power of action (kriyāśakti) manifests time and space as ‘paths’ (adhvas). The path of space is a means of unifying and traversing insubstantial and substantial forms such as the intellect, the altar or image, the void. Despite this diversity, all these forms abide in consciousness and have their place of repose (viśrānti) in consciousness. ↩︎

  2. On the path, all forms shine internally and externally through the outlets of consciousness; this is how they are created and manifested. Consciousness, in its autonomy, shines as both the knowing subject and its object. The variety of objects, though not different from the knowing subject, appears as if different from it. ↩︎

  3. The aspirant attains the nature of Bhairava by perceiving everything, from the all-consuming fire (kālāgni) said to abide at the bottom of the earth, to the highest principle, i.e., anāśritaśiva, in ascending order and unifying them into the singularity of I-Consciousness. [[237]] ↩︎

  4. Worlds of a variegated nature are included under the principle of earth. The sphere under which they shine is called the sphere of Brahmā. Similarly, other spheres, e.g., the sphere of prakr̥ti, the sphere of māyā, and the sphere of śakti, are wider than the preceding one. All the principles from water to prakr̥ti come under the sphere of prakr̥ti. All the principles from puruṣa to māyā come under the sphere of māyā. The extent of the sphere of śakti extends up to the Sadāśiva principle. Thus, the number of spheres (andas) is four. The spheres are the masses of substance which have fallen from the state of energy. ↩︎

  5. According to this system, there are ten ‘guardians of the quarters’ in accordance with the number of directions. The guardians of each direction are composed of a group of ten, consisting of a superintending deity and nine subordinates. Taken as ten groups of ten guardians, the total number becomes 100 deities; therefore, they are known as the “Hundred Rudras" (Satarudras). ↩︎

  6. This system envisions a limitless number of ‘worlds’ made from and dominated by each principle. These worlds exist so that souls which have not received the Śaiva form of initiation, but have attained a lower form of perfection through a continuous practice of concentration, receive a body and sense organs best suited for experiencing pleasure and pain in a particular world, and may finally attain identity with the Lord.+++(4)+++ Every world has superintending deities who are called the Lords of that world. ↩︎

  7. Located in the intellect principle (buddhi tattva) are eight ‘worlds’ (bhuvana), each dominated by a class of heavenly beings or deity: pisacas, raksasas, yakśas, gandharvas, Indra, Soma, Prajāpati, and Brahmā. ↩︎

  8. According to the Svacchanda Tantra, the names of the worlds under prakr̥ti are: Akr̥ta, kr̥ta, Vaibhava, Brahma, Vaiṣṇava, Kaumāra, ‘belonging to Uma’ (Auma), and ‘belonging to Srīkaṇṭha.’

    Kṣemarāja explains that those who have practiced yoga with renunciation attain the ‘world’ (bhuvana) named Akr̥ta, while those who practiced karmayoga reach the bhuvana named kr̥ta.

    Similarly, those who practiced nirmāṇayoga go to the world of [[238]] r̥bhus.

    The abode of Brahmā is attainable to those who practice yoga with conviction that puruṣa is one universal soul.

    The abode of Viṣnu is the final resort for those who strictly practiced yoga according to Vaiṣṇava tradition.

    Those who practice yoga maintaining celibacy (brahmacarya) go to the abode of Kaumāra world.

    The abodes above this, named Auma and Srīkaṇṭha, are the abodes of those who practice yoga according to the Śaiva doctrine. ↩︎

  9. There are seven groups of eight deities who preside over different principles.

    • The first is called patyaṣṭaka, the group of eight Lords beginning with Lakulisa and ending with Amaresa.
    • The second is called Guhyaṣṭaka who are the Lords of fire; the first of them is Bhairava.
    • The third group is Atiguhyașttaka, which rules over the principle named wind (vayu).
    • The fourth group of eight is called Pavitrāstaka, with Sthāṇu, etc., who rule over the principle called ākāśa.
    • The fifth group of eight, beginning with Sthūla and ending with Chagalāṇda, rules over ahamkāra. The sixth group of eight divinities called Yonyaṣṭaka rules over the intellect.
    • The seventh group of eight is called Yogāṣṭaka which rules over prakr̥ti.
     ↩︎

  10. The eleven worlds of the Rudras are as follows: Vāma, Bhīma, Ugra, Bhava, Isa, Ekavīra, Pracanda, Gauri, Aja, Ananta, and Ekaśiva. ↩︎

  11. The names of the worlds in impure vidyā, kalā, and niyati are Krodheśa and Canda, Samvarta and Jyoti, Sūra and Pancānta, respectively. ↩︎

  12. The worlds under kāla and mayā are as follows: Ekavīra, śikhi, Śrīkantha in kāla, while in māyā, Mahātejas, Vāma, Bhava, Udbhava, Ekapinga and īsāna, Bhuvanesa and Anguṣṭha. ↩︎

  13. In pure vidyā principle there are five worlds. They are: Halahalarudra, Krodha, Ambika, Aghora, and Yama. ↩︎

  14. Every principle is composed of subtle elements (kalās). They are called nivr̥tti, pratiṣṭhā, vidyā, śāntā, and sāntātītā. The number of worlds in nivr̥ttikalā is sixteen; in pratiṣṭhā the number is fifty six; in vidyā the number is twenty-eight; in śāntā the number is eighteen. There are no worlds in śāntātītā. Thus, the total number of the worlds in all five kalās is one hundred eighteen. [[239]] ↩︎
