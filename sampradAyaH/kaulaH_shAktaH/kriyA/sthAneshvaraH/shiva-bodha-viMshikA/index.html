TWENTY STANZAS ON THE REALIZATION OF ŚIVA

विस्तारः (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)

svabhāvaḥ (m. nom. sg.), essential nature; sarvabhāvānāṃ (m. gen. pl.), of all that exists; svatantraṃ (n. nom. sg.), autonomous; cit, consciousness; śivaḥ, Śiva; advayaḥ (m. nom. sg.), nondual; kalpitaṃ (ppp. n. nom. sg.), what is imagined; paratantraṃ (n. nom. sg.), mutually constituted; ca (indecl.), and; svabhāvāt (m. abla. sg.), absolute essential nature; na, not; atiricyate (verb. pres. ātman. 3rd sg. atir+ric), it exceeds.

स्वभावः सर्वभावानां
स्वतन्त्रा चित्शिवोऽद्वयः। कल्पितं परतन्त्रं च
स्वभावान् नातिरिच्यते॥१॥

The nondual Śiva, autonomous consciousness, is the essential nature of all that exists. Even what is imagined and what is mutually constituted does not exceed the absolute essential nature.

śivabodhaviṃśikā Twenty Stanzas on the Realization of Śiva

saṃvidrūpaṃ svataḥsiddhaṃ bhāvayan kramaśo ‘dvayam ।
draṣṭāraṃ darśanaṃ dṛśyaṃ śivaṃ buddhvā vimucyate ॥2॥

saṃvidrūpaṃ (n. acc. sg.), nature of consciousness; svataḥsiddhaṃ (n. acc. sg.), self-established; bhāvayan (nom. sg.), he recognizes; kramaśaḥ, successively; advayam (n. acc. sg.), nondual; draṣṭāraṃ (n. acc. sg.), perceiver; darśanaṃ (n. acc. sg.), perception; dṛśyaṃ (n. acc. sg.), perceived; śivaṃ (m. acc. sg.), Śiva nature; buddhvā , when one meditates; vimucyate (3rd. sg. pres. ātman.), one attains liberation. संविद्रू पं स्वतःसिद्धं भावयन्कक्रमशोऽद्वयम्। द्रष्टारं दर्शनं दृश्यं शिवं बुद्ध्वद्ध वा विमुच्यते॥२॥ One attains liberation when one meditates on the self-established nature of consciousness by successively recognizing the nondual Śiva nature in the triadic structure of the perceiver, perception, and what is perceived.

annaprāṇamayaṃ dehaṃ cinmātramiti bhāvayet ।
kheṣu khaṃ salilaṃ toye tathā dehe śivaḥ sthitaḥ ॥3॥

anna-prāṇa-mayaṃ (m. acc. sg.), made of food (anna) and vitality (prāṇa); dehaṃ (m. acc. sg.), body; cinmātram (n. acc. sg.), consciousness; iti, as merely; bhāvayet (3rd sg. opt. parasm. ), one should meditate on; kheṣu (n. loc. pl. -kha), in the sensory faculties; khaṃ (n. nom. sg. -kha), open sky; salilaṃ (n. nom. sg.), water; toye (n. loc. sg.), in water; tathā, (indecl.) such, the way; dehe (m. loc. sg.), in the body; śivaḥ (m. nom. sg.), Śiva; sthitaḥ (m. nom. sg.), is established. शिवकायभावना śivakāyabhāvanā One should meditate on the body made of food and vitality as merely consciousness alone. Śiva is established in the body the way the same open sky of consciousness is established in the sensory faculties or the way water is mixed in water. अन्नप्राणमयं देहं चिन्मात्रमिति भावयेत्। खेषु खं सलिलं तोये तथा देहे शिवः स्थितः॥३॥ प्रथमा भावना prathamā bhāvanā First visualization Visualization of the body as Śiva

śivo rūpaṃ raso gandhaḥ sparśaḥ śabdaś ca cinmayaḥ ।
bāhyam indriyagrāhyaṃ yat sarvaṃ śivamayaṃ tataḥ ॥4॥

śivaḥ (m. nom. sg.), Śiva; rūpaṃ (n. nom. sg.), form; rasaḥ (m. nom. sg.), taste; gandhaḥ (m. nom. sg.), smell; sparśaḥ (m. nom. sg.), touch; śabdaḥ (m. nom. sg.), sound; ca (indecl.), and; cinmayaḥ (m. nom. sg.), of the character of consciousness; bāhyam, external; indriyagrāhyaṃ, object of sensory faculty; yat (indecl.), that; sarvaṃ, all; śivamayaṃ (m. acc. sg.), of the character of Śiva; tataḥ (indecl.), therefore. शिवतन्मात्रभावना śivatanmātrabhāvanā All form, taste, smell, touch, and sound are Śiva and they are of the character of consciousness alone. All that is external and an object of the sensory faculty is therefore of the character of Śiva. शिवो रूपं रसो गन्धः स्पर्शशः शब्दश्च चिन्मयः। बाह्यमिन्द्रियग्राह्यं यत्सर्वं शिवमयं ततः॥४॥ द्वितीया भावना dvitīyā bhāvanā Second visualization Visualization of the subtle elements as Śiva darśanaṃ cidrasāpūraṃ dṛśyaṃ citi vikalpitam । bāhyam ābhyantaraṃ sarvaṃ śivam ekaṃ cidātmakam ॥5॥ darśanaṃ (n. acc. sg.), seeing; cit, modes of consciousness; rasa, essence; āpūraṃ (m. acc. sg.), it is soaked; dṛsyaṃ (n. acc. sg.), what is seen; citi (n. loc. sg.), in consciousness; vikalpitam (n. acc. sg.), conceptualized; bāhyam, external; ābhyantaraṃ, internal; sarvaṃ, all; śivam (m. acc. sg.), Śiva; ekaṃ, alone; cidātmakam (m. acc. sg.), of the character of consciousness. दृग्श्य दृ शिवता dṛgdṛśyaśivatā [One should visualize that] our modes of consciousness are soaked with the rasa of consciousness and what we see is conceptualized within consciousness. All the external and the internal are Śiva alone, of the character of consciousness. दर्शनं चिद्रसापूरं दृश्यं चिति विकल्पितम्। बाह्यमाभ्यन्तरं सर्वं शिवमेकं चिदात्मकम्॥५॥ तृतीया भावना tṛtīyā bhāvanā Third visualization Śivahood of the seen and the seeing sarvaṃ cid iti vijñānaṃ cidbhūmāv asti kalpitam । astināstivikalpānāṃ grāhakaś citi bhāvitaḥ ॥6॥ sarvaṃ, all; cid iti, consciousness only; vijñānaṃ (n. nom. sg.), knowledge; cit, consciousness; bhūmau (m. loc. Sg. - bhūmi), in the foundation; asti (3rd sg. present parasm. √as), it is; kalpitam (n. acc. sg.), conceptualization; asti (3rd sg. present parasm. √as), it is; na (indecl.), not; asti (3rd sg. present parasm. √as), it is; vikalpānāṃ (f. gen. pl.), of conceptualizations; grāhakaḥ (m. nom. sg.), subject; citi (n. loc. sg.), within consciousness; bhāvitaḥ (m. nom. sg.), is conceived. चिच्चिन्मयता ciccinmayatā Even the knowledge that all is consciousness only is envisioned within the foundation of consciousness. The subject of conceptualizations such as “is” or “is not” is conceived within consciousness. सर्वं चिदिति विज्ञानं चिद्मावस्ति कल्पितम्। भू अस्तिनास्तिविकल्पानां ग्राहकश्चिति भावितः॥६॥ चतर्ुथी भावना caturthī bhāvanā Fourth visualization Consciousness only of cognition prācyavīcipratīcyādidigvibhāgo ‘sti cinmayaḥ । citkalpitavikalpānyā nāsti digdeśavastutā ॥7॥ prācī, east; avīci, north; pratīci, west; ādi , and so on; dik, direction; vibhāgaḥ (m. nom. sg.), differentiation; asti (3rd sg. present parasm. √as), it is; cinmayaḥ (m. nom. sg.), consisting of pure consciousness; cit, consciousness; kalpita (ppp. n. acc. sg.), conceptualization; vikalpa, substantiality of direction or space; anyā (f. nom. sg.), other; na (indecl.), not; asti (3rd sg. present parasm. √as), it is; dik, direction; deśa, space; vastutā (f. nom. sg.), substantiality. दिग्देशचिदात्मता digdeśacidātmatā The differentiation in terms of east, north, west, and so on, is merely consciousness. There is no substantiality of direction or space other than conceptualization made by consciousness. प्राच्यवीचिप्रतीच्यादिदिग्विभागोऽस्ति चिन्मयः। चित्कल्पितविकल्पान्या नास्ति दिग्देशवस्तुता॥७॥ पञ्चमी भावना pañcamī bhāvanā Fifth visualization Consciousness only of space and directions yathā bandhas tathā muktiś cinmātraparikalpitā । śivo baddhaś śivo muktaḥ svātantryān nātiricyate ॥8॥ yathā, just like; bandhaḥ (m. nom. sg.), bondage; tathā, so also; muktiḥ (f. nom. sg.) liberation; cinmātra, consciousness; parikalpitā (f. nom. sg.), imagined; śivaḥ (m. nom. sg.), Śiva; baddhaḥ (m. nom. sg.), bound; śivaḥ (m. nom. sg.), Śiva; muktaḥ (m. nom. sg.), liberated; svātantryāt (n. abl. sg.), freedom; na (indecl.), not; atiricyate (verb. pres. ātman. 3rd sg. ati+ric), it exceeds. निर्ववाणशिवात्मता nirvāṇaśivātmatā Just like bondage is imagined within consciousness, so also is liberation. The very Śiva is bound, and the very Śiva is liberated. Nothing exceeds freedom. यथा बन्धस्तथा मुक्तिश्चिन्मात्रपरिकल्पिता। शिवो बद्धश्शिवो मुक्तः स्वातन्तत्र्यान्नातिरिच्यते॥८॥ षष्ठी भावना ṣaṣṭhī bhāvanā Sixth visualization Śiva nature and liberation samudeti yataḥ kāmaḥ rūpaṃ yatrāsti bimbitam । līyante yatra vai kāmās teṣāṃ bhūmiḥ śivo mataḥ ॥9॥ samudeti (sam-ut-eti, 3rd sg. present, √i ), emerges; yataḥ (indecl.), since; kāmaḥ (m. nom. sg.), desire; rūpaṃ (n. nom. sg.), form; yatra (indecl.), wherever; asti (3rd sg. present parasm. √as), it is; bimbitam (n. nom. sg.), reflected; līyante (3rd pl. ppp), they are dissolved yatra (indecl.), wherever; vā (indecl.), or; kāmāḥ (m. nom. pl.), desires; teṣāṃ (tat, f. gen. pl.), of these; bhūmiḥ (f. nom. sg.), foundation; śivaḥ (m. nom. sg.), Śiva; mataḥ (m. nom. sg. ppp.), understood. कामशिवात्मता kāmaśivātmatā The foundation where desire emerges or where desired forms are reflected or where desires dissolve, Śiva is the foundation of these [foundations]. समुदेति यतः कामः रूपं यत्रास्ति बिम्बितम्। लीयन्ते यत्र वै कामास्तेषां भूमिः शिवो मतः॥९॥ सप्तमी भावना saptamī bhāvanā Seventh visualization Śiva nature of desire svabhāvaḥ sarvabhāvānām udayāstavivarjitaḥ । sa śivaḥ niḥsvabhāvo ‘pi svabhāvaś cinmayo ‘dvayaḥ ॥10॥ svabhāvaḥ (m. nom. sg.), essential nature; sarva, all; bhāva, nature; anāmudayāsta vivarjitaḥ (m. nom. sg.), devoid; saḥ (m. nom. sg.), that; śivaḥ (m. nom. sg.), Śiva; niḥsvabhāvaḥ (m. nom. sg.), not having any essential nature; api (indecl.), still; svabhāvaḥ (m. nom. sg.), essential nature; cinmayaḥ (m. nom. sg.), consciousness; advayaḥ (n. nom. sg.), nondual. निःस्वभावशिवात्मता niḥsvabhāvaśivātmatā The essential nature of all the entities is devoid of arising and collapsing. This very nature is Śiva. Even what you say as not having any essential nature is still an essential nature. This is nondual consciousness. स्वभावः सर्वभावानामुदयास्तविवर्जितः। स शिवः निःस्वभावोऽपि स्वभावश्चिन्मयोऽद्वयः॥१०॥ अष्टमी भावना aṣṭamī bhāvanā Eighth visualization Śiva nature of no intrinsic nature kṣaṇikaṃ śāśvataṃ caite vijñānaparikalpite । bhāvo ‘bhāvaḥ svabhāvau dvau cinmātraparikalpitau ॥11॥ kṣaṇikaṃ (n.), momentary; śāśvataṃ (n.), permanence; ca (indecl.), and; ete , (n. nom. dual), these two; vijñāna (n.), mind; parikalpite (n. nom. sg. caus. ppp.), conceptualized; bhāvaḥ (m. nom. sg.), being; abhāvaḥ (m. nom. sg.), nonbeing; svabhāvau (n. nom. dual), two natures; dvau (m. nom. dual), both; cinmātra (n.), consciousness; parikalpitau (n. nom. dual caus. ppp.), imagined. द्वयशिवात्मता dvayaśivātmatā Both momentariness and permanence are conceptualized by the mind. Both being and nonbeing are the natures imagined merely by consciousness. क्षणिकं शाश्वतं चैते विज्ञानपरिकल्पिते। भावोऽभावः स्वभावौ द्वौ चिन्मात्रपरिकल्पितौ॥११॥ नवमी भावना navamī bhāvanā Ninth visualization Śiva nature of the dichotomy mūlāgramadhyaśūnyaṃ khaṃ dikkālādhāram avyayam । ādyantarahitād ātmabodhāt tan nātiricyate ॥12॥ mūla (n), basis; agra (n.), end; madhya (m.), middle; śūnyaṃ (n. acc. sg.), void; khaṃ (n. acc. sg.), space; dik (f.), direction; kāla (m.), time; ādhāram (m. acc. sg.), foundation; avyayam (n. nom. sg.), changeless; ādi, beginning; antara, difference; hitāt (n. abl. sg. ppp), from having; ātmabodhāt (m. abl. sg.), from self-awareness; tat (pron.), this; na (indecl.), not; atiricyate (3rd sg. present ātman. ati-√ric), it separates. आधारशिवात्मता ādhāraśivātmatā [The void in the form of] Consciousness is changeless, is the foundation of space and time, and has no basis, middle, or ending. This [consciousness] is not separate from self-awareness that [also] has neither the beginning nor the ending. मूलाग्रमध्यशून्यं खं दिक्कालाधारमव्ययम्। आद्यन्तरहितादात्मबोधात्तन्नातिरिच्यते॥१२॥ दशमी भावना daśamī bhāvanā Tenth visualization Śiva nature of the basis gamane darśane dhyāne kartṛkāryavikalpite । ekaṃ caitanyam ābhāti bhedaḥ salilacandravat ॥13॥ gamane (n. loc. sg.), in going; darśane (n. loc. sg.), in seeing; dhyāne (n. loc. sg.), in thinking; kartṛ, agent; kārya, action; vikalpite (n. loc. sg.), in conceptualization; ekaṃ (n.), singular; caitanyam, consciousness; ābhāti (3rd. sg. parasm. pres. ā-√bhā), it manifests; bhedaḥ (m. nom. sg.), difference; salila, water; candravat, similar to the moon. क्रियाशिवात्मता kriyāśivātmatā In [the acts of] going, seeing, or thinking, where there is conceptualization in terms of agent and action, there manifests merely a singular consciousness. The difference is similar to the moon [reflected] in water. गमने दर्शने ध्याने कर्ततृकार्यविकल्पिते। एकं चैतन्यमाभाति भेदः सलिलचन्द्रवत्॥१३॥ एकादशी भावना ekādaśī bhāvanā Eleventh visualization Śiva nature of movement śāśvataṃ kṣaṇikaṃ yadyan niḥsvabhāvaṃ nirātmakam । śāśvataṃ kṣaṇikaṃ yadyan niḥsvabhāvaṃ nirātmakam ॥14॥ śāśvataṃ (n. nom. sg.), permanent; kṣaṇikaṃ (n. nom. sg.), momentary; yadyat, all of that; niḥsvabhāvaṃ (n. nom. sg.), void of properties; nirātmakam (n. nom. sg.), that which lacks its own intrinsic nature; tat (indecl.), that; saṃvit, consciousness; bhittim (f. acc. sg.), foundation; ālambya, resting; śivātmani (n. Loc. sg.), in the nature of Śiva; avabhāsate (3rd sg. pres. ātman. √bhās) it manifests. सर्वभावशिवात्मता sarvabhāvaśivātmatā That which is permanent [such as sky] or that which is momentary [such as duration of photon in a single space], or that which lacks its own intrinsic nature [such as mirage], all of that manifests in the self of the nature of Śiva by resting on the foundation of consciousness. शाश्वतं क्षणिकं यद्यन्नििःस्वभावं निरात्मकम्। तत्संविद्भित्तिमालम्ब्य शिवात्मन्यवभासते॥१४॥ द्वादशी भावना dvādaśī bhāvanā Twelveth visualization Śiva nature of all modes [of being and non-being] bhāvānām udayāstau ca bodhodayalayau tayoḥ । sarvaṃ ciddhāmni nikṣipya svatantro viharec chivaḥ ॥15॥ bhāvānam, what exists; udaya, rise; astau (m. nom. dual ppp.), collapse; ca (indecl.), and; bodha udaya, rise; layau (m. nom. dual), collapse; tayoḥ (m. gen. dual), of those two; sarvaṃ, all; cit, consciousness; dhāmni (m. loc. sg.), within the foundation; nikṣipya , throwing; svatantraḥ, freely; viharet (3rd sg. opt. √vihṛ), he should stroll; śivaḥ (m. nom. sg.), śiva/consciousness. सर्वधर्मशिवता sarvadharmaśivatā One should freely stroll in the world by situating everything within the foundation of consciousness, both the rise and collapse of what exists and the rise and collapse of consciousness of those modes [of arising and collapsing]. भावानामुदयास्तौ च बोधोदयलयौ तयोः। सर्वं चिद्धाम्नि निक्षिप्य स्वतन्त्रो विहरेच्छिवः॥१५॥ त्रयोदशी भावना trayodaśī bhāvanā Thirteenth visualization Śiva nature of all dharmas pañcabhūtānyahaṃkāro ravīndū cāṣṭamūrtayaḥ । pratyakṣaṃ śiva ityetadbhāvayan śivatodayaḥ ॥16॥ pañcabhūtāni (n. nom. pl.), five elements; ahaṃkāraḥ (m. nom. sg.), ego; ravi (m.), sun; indū (m. nom. dual), moon; ca (indecl.), and; aṣṭa, eight; mūrtayaḥ (m. nom. pl.), bodies; pratyakṣaṃ, manifest; śiva, śiva; iti (quotation mark); etat, this; bhāvayan (m. nom. sg.), regarding, with; śivatā, śiva nature; udayaḥ (m. nom. sg.), the arise. प्रकटशिवता prakaṭaśivatā The five elements, the ego, the moon, and the sun: visualize these eight manifest bodies as being directly the apprehended forms of Śiva. With this visualization, there arises Śiva nature. पञ्चभूतान्यहंकारो रवीन्दूचाष्टमूर्तयः। प्रत्यक्षं शिव इत्येतद्भावयन् शिवतोदयः॥१६॥ चतर्ुशी भावना ्द caturdaśī bhāvanā Fourteenth visualization Śiva nature of all the manifest bodies atītaṃ vartamānaṃ cānāgataṃ ca citi sthitam । parasparaviruddhatvaṃ na śive satatodite ॥17॥ atītaṃ (n. nom. sg.), past; vartamānaṃ (n. nom. sg.), present; ca (indecl.), and; anāgataṃ (n. nom. sg.), future; ca (indecl.), and; citi (f. loc. sg.), in consciousness; sthitam (n. nom. sg.), situated; paraspara, mutual; viruddhatvaṃ (n. acc. sg.), contradiction; na (indecl.), no; śive (m. loc. sg.), in Śiva; satata, uninterruptedly; udite (m. loc. sg.), in arising. सर्वकालशिवता sarvakālaśivatā The past, the present, or the future, are all simultaneously present in consciousness. Śiva, who is uninterruptedly arising, has no mutual contradiction. अतीतं वर्तमानं चानागतं च चिति स्थितम्। परस्परविरुद्धत्वं न शिवे सततोदिते॥१७॥ पञ्चदशी भावना pañcadaśī bhāvanā Fifteenth visualization Śiva nature of all modes of time sarvaṃ saṃyogajaṃ bāhyaṃ vastu yad yat prakāśate । saṃyogajanyatāṃ dhyātvā svabhāvaṃ citi viśramet ॥18॥ sarvaṃ, all; saṃyogajaṃ (n. acc. sg.), composite; bāhyaṃ, external; vastu, thing, object; yadyat, all of that; prakāśate (3rd. sg. ātmanep. pres. pra-√kāś), it manifests; saṃyogajanyatāṃ (n. acc. sg.), composed; dhyātvā, by meditating upon; svabhāvaṃ (m. acc. sg.), intrinsic nature; citi (f. loc. sg.), in consciousness; viśramet (3rd sg. parasm. vidhil. vi-√śram), one should rest. सं योगशिवता saṃyogaśivatā All that manifests as the external is a composite [made of contact]. By meditating upon their intrinsic nature as ‘composed’ [or due to contact], one rests on pure consciousness. सर्वं सं योगजं बाह्यं वस्तु यद्यत्प्रकाशते। सं योगजन्यतां ध्यात्वा स्वभावं चिति विश्रमेत्॥१८॥ षोडशी भावना ṣoḍaśī bhāvanā Sixteenth visualization Śiva nature of the [composites made of] contact saṃyogasya vikalpa tvād vikalpānāṃ cidāśrayāt । vikalpān budbudākārān vilayet citi sāgare ॥19॥ saṃyogasya (m. gen. sg.), contact; vikalpatvāt (n. abl. sg.), from variety; vikalpānāṃ (n. nom. sg.), mental constructs; cit, consciousness; āśrayāt (m. abl. sg.), from resting; vikalpān (m. nom. pl.), mental constructs; budduda, bubble; ākārān (m. acc. pl.), character; vilayet (3rd. sg. opt. √vil), one should dissolve; citi (f. loc. sg.), into consciousness; sāgare (n. loc. sg.), in the ocean. विकल्पशिवता vikalpaśivatā Even contact is a form of mental construct. All the mental constructs rest on consciousness. One should dissolve the mental constructs of the character of bubbles into the consciousness of the character of the ocean. सं योगस्य विकल्पत्वाद्विकल्पानां चिदाश्रयात्। विकल्पान्बबुद्दाकारा बु न्विलयेत् चिति सागरे॥१९॥ सप्तदशी भावना saptadaśī bhāvanā Seventeenth visualization Śiva nature of conceptualization atyantābhāvarūpaṃ vānyonyābhāvasvabhāvakam । abhāvaṃ satataṃ dhyāyet śivasadbhāvabhāvitam ॥20॥ atyanta, absolute; abhāva, absence; rūpaṃ (n. acc. sg.), form; vā (indecl.), or; anyonya, mutual; abhāva, absence; svabhāvakam, essential nature; abhāvaṃ (m. acc. sg.), absence; satataṃ, constantly; dhyāyet (3rd. sg. parasm. vidhil. √dhyā); śivasadbhāva, essential nature of Śiva; bhāvitam (n. acc. sg.), imagined upon. अभावशिवता abhāvaśivatā One should constantly meditate on absence whether in the form of absolute absence [or prasajya type negation] or mutual absence [paryudasa type negation] as imagined upon the essential nature of Śiva. अत्यन्ताभावरूपं वान्योन्याभावस्वभावकम्। अभावं सततं ध्यायेत् शिवसद्भावभावितम्॥२०॥ अष्टादशी भावना aṣṭādaśī bhāvanā Eighteenth visualization Śiva nature of non-being svabhāvasya svabhāvatvaṃ yadālambya prakāśate । svātantryaṃ citsvarūpaṃ tat śivatattvam anuttaram ॥21॥ svabhāvasya (m. gen. sg.), of the intrinsic nature; svabhāvatvaṃ (n. nom. sg.), the character of; yat (indecl.), that; ālambya, sustaining; prakāśate (3rd. sg. ātmanep. pres. pra-√kāś), it manifests; svātantryaṃ (n. nom. sg.), absolute freedom; cit, consciousness; svarūpaṃ (n. acc. sg.), nature; tat (indecl.), this; śivatattvam, Śiva; anuttaram (m. acc. sg.), unsurpassable. स्वभावशिवता svabhāvaśivatā It is the absolute freedom of the character of consciousness that is called Śiva the unsurpassable and even the intrinsic nature [of everything] is dependent upon this [very freedom of the nature of consciousness] for its manifestation. स्वभावस्य स्वभावत्वं यदालम्ब्य प्रकाशते। स्वातन्तत्र्यं चित्स्वरूपं तत् शिवतत्त्वमनुत्तरम्॥२१॥ नवदशी भावना navadaśī bhāvanā Nineteenth visualization Śiva nature of the inherent nature svātantryaṃ sarvabhāvānāṃ yad adhiṣṭhānam ucyate । sarvatra satataṃ tasya sphāraḥ sarvaṃ śivas tataḥ ॥22॥ svātantryaṃ (n. nom. sg.), freedom; sarvabhāvānāṃ (m. gen. pl.), of all entities; yat (indecl.), that; adhiṣṭhānam (n. acc. sg.), foundation; ucyate (3rd sg. ātman. pres.√vac), it is expressed; sarvatra, everywhere; satataṃ, constantly; tasya, of that; sphāraḥ (m. nom. sg.), large, great; sarvaṃ, all; śivaḥ (m. nom. sg.), Śiva; tataḥ, therefore. स्वातन्त्र्यशिवता svātantryaśivatā The freedom that is considered the foundation of all the entities is being expressed everywhere in all the modes of time and therefore everything is of the nature of Śiva. स्वातन्तत्र्यं सर्वभावानां यदधिष्ठानमुच्यते। सर्वत्र सततं तस्य स्फारः सर्वं शिवस्ततः॥२२॥ वििंशी भावना viṃśī bhāvanā Twentieth visualization Śiva nature of freedom guror vākpuṣpa nicayamākalayya nigumphitā । srag iyaṃ śaṃkarasyāstu gaurīprītyupapādikā ॥ guroḥ (m. gen. sg.), of guru; vāc, word; puṣpa, flower; nicayam, collection; ākalayya, offering; nigumphitā (f. nom. sg.), it is made of, it is woven; sragiyaṃ (n. nom. sg.), rosary; śaṃkarasya (m. gen. sg.), of Śaṅkara; astu (3rd sg. imper. parasm. √as), may it be; gaurī, Pārvatī; prīti, love; upapādikā (f. nom. sg.), she who causes/instigate. गुरोर्ववाक्ष्पपु निचयमाकलय्य निगुम्फफिता। स्रगियं शं करस्यास्तु गौरीप्रीत्युपपादिका॥ This rosary is made of a collection of flowers of the nature of the words of Guru. May this rosary instigate love in Pārvatī for Śaṅkara. ॥ ityācāryasthāneśvarapraṇītā śivabodhaviṃśikā ॥ ॥इत्याचार्यस्थानेश्वरप्रणीता शिवबोधविं शिका॥ Thus ends twenty stanzas for the realization of Śiva, composed by Ācārya Sthāneśvara.
