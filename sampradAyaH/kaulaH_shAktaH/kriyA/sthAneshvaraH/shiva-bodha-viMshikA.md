स्वभावः सर्वभावानां  
स्वतन्त्रा चित्शिवोऽद्वयः।
कल्पितं परतन्त्रं च  
स्वभावान् नातिरिच्यते॥१॥

The nondual Śiva, autonomous consciousness, is the essential
nature of all that exists. Even what is imagined and what is
mutually constituted does not exceed the absolute essential
nature.

Twenty Stanzas on the Realization of Śiva

saṃvidrūpaṃ svataḥsiddhaṃ bhāvayan kramaśo ‘dvayam ।  
draṣṭāraṃ darśanaṃ dṛśyaṃ śivaṃ buddhvā vimucyate ॥2॥

saṃvidrūpaṃ (n. acc. sg.), nature of consciousness; svataḥsiddhaṃ
(n. acc. sg.), self-established; bhāvayan (nom. sg.), he recognizes;
kramaśaḥ, successively; advayam (n. acc. sg.), nondual; draṣṭāraṃ
(n. acc. sg.), perceiver; darśanaṃ (n. acc. sg.), perception; dṛśyaṃ (n.
acc. sg.), perceived; śivaṃ (m. acc. sg.), Śiva nature; buddhvā , when
one meditates; vimucyate (3rd. sg. pres. ātman.), one attains liberation.
संविद्रू
पं स्वतःसिद्धं भावयन्कक्रमशोऽद्वयम्।
द्रष्टारं दर्शनं दृश्यं शिवं बुद्ध्वद्ध वा विमुच्यते॥२॥
One attains liberation when one meditates on the self-established nature of consciousness by successively recognizing the
nondual Śiva nature in the triadic structure of the perceiver,
perception, and what is perceived.

 शिवकायभावना
śivakāyabhāvanā
One should meditate on the body made of food and vitality as
merely consciousness alone. Śiva is established in the body the
way the same open sky of consciousness is established in the
sensory faculties or the way water is mixed in water.

शिवतन्मात्रभावना
śivatanmātrabhāvanā
All form, taste, smell, touch, and sound are Śiva and they are
of the character of consciousness alone. All that is external and
an object of the sensory faculty is therefore of the character
of Śiva.

collapsing. This very nature is Śiva. Even what you say as not
having any essential nature is still an essential nature. This is
nondual consciousness.

सं योगशिवता
saṃyogaśivatā
All that manifests as the external is a composite [made of contact]. By meditating upon their intrinsic nature as ‘composed’
[or due to contact], one rests on pure consciousness.

विकल्पशिवता
vikalpaśivatā
Even contact is a form of mental construct. All the mental
constructs rest on consciousness. One should dissolve the
mental constructs of the character of bubbles into the
consciousness of the character of the ocean.

abhāvaśivatā
One should constantly meditate on absence whether in the
form of absolute absence [or prasajya type negation] or mutual
absence [paryudasa type negation] as imagined upon the
essential nature of Śiva.

svabhāvaśivatā
It is the absolute freedom of the character of consciousness that
is called Śiva the unsurpassable and even the intrinsic nature

स्वातन्त्र्यशिवता

The freedom that is considered the foundation of all the entities
is being expressed everywhere in all the modes of time and
therefore everything is of the nature of Śiva.
